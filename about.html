Technology

As a young child I was always using my parents Tandy desktop computer playing games and using the Encarta Encyclopedia to learn new information. From there it only grew into wanting to learn and understand more about the technology field. I was building, repairing and troubleshooting PCs consistently for family and friends. I also started repairing and troubleshooting mobile devices. I would fix and repair cracked screens and replace other faulty components for friends. The list goes on.

Medical Simulation

I had no idea that four years of my life would be spent interacting with sophisticated medical technology. I was a Lead Installation Technician at Kb Port and learned a lot about not only computers but technology and how it applies to the medical field. I have an extensive knowledge base and troubleshooting skill with popular healthcare manikins; specifically, Laerdal, Gaumard and Metiman simulators. I also developed other various skills including audio and video installation and troubleshooting. It also taught me to be well prepared. Our jobs were sometimes up to 2,600 miles away from our shop. We had to be prepared and packed with our tools, equipment, and other supplies.
